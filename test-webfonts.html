Font test כי חסר לי פונטים

I can’t find a practical free font containing both Hebrew and Latin characters, so I’m hoping that this will prove adequate. As an example of running text using both alphabets, I have adapted some text from my favorite attested sample of Yeshivish, Rabbi Benzion Klatzko’s The Kuntrus, and putting some of it in a serif font and some in sans-serif.

I welcome spelling corrections from the more fluent.

Making a מישמר

I once heard from a ראש ישיבה that it’s כדי for a בחור to make a מישמר once a week, even if it means he will be tired the next day. This is in order for him to be מחנך himself that לימוד התורה is worth being מושר נפש for.

I used to make my מישמר on Thursday night, but when I saw how אויסמנש I was by Friday morning סדר, I switched it to Friday night. In ארץ ישראל, I switched back to Thursday night in order to daven וותיקין at the כותל the next morning. א מחייל!

Friday’s—Going to Town [testing a sans-serif פונט]

There are many reasons that בכורים go to town. Buying soda, pickles, hot sauce or anything else לכבוד שבת. Picking up or dropping off clothes at the cleaners. A haircut (if you don’t trust the yeshiva barber) or a doctor and dentist appointment can also be arranged for Friday. Just heed my very real warning. The world is a שמוציגע place and one trip into town can be עוקר a week’s worth of learning and עלייה. What a heavy price to pay for a jar of gerkins! My humble recommendation? Never go alone. והמבין יבין.
