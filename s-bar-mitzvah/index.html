The synagogue

Congregation Kadimah-Toras Moshe (KTM), located at 113 Washington Street in Brighton (near the corner of Washington Street and Commonwealth Avenue) is a small Orthodox synagogue.

Some points of etiquette

  • Men and women are seated separately for prayer, with a room divider (mechitza). With a few exceptions, only Jewish males age 13 and up take part in running services. Unless you are the first to arrive, it should be immediately obvious which side is which. Siddurim (prayer books) and chumashim (Bibles) are available on the shelves immediately inside the sanctuary doors.
  • Men (even non-Jewish men) should have their heads covered with a kippah (yarmulke, skullcap) or a hat approriate for a place of worship. Kippot will be available in a basket in the lobby. Jewish men may wear taleisim (prayer shawls) or not, according to their tradition, but you must wear one if you are participating in the liturgy or given a synagogue honor.
  • It is the norm in our synagogue for married women to wear a hat or other hair covering, but not to wear a kippah or a tallis. However, you should do what works for you.
  • Dress is business-casual or better, and modest appropriate for the wearer’s age and respect for a place of worship. For men, jackets and/or ties are common but not universal. Most adult women wear the sort of skirts and blouses you would wear to be a secretary in an office with periodic client contact, and flat shoes; some wear more formal businesswear, and some wear pantsuits. Kids wear whatever their parents can convince them to wear. Sometimes the best they can manage is pajamas.
  • In order to enhance our members’ concentration on the holiness of the Sabbath day, please silence your cell phone and do not use it, or cameras, or similar equipment, on the synagogue grounds, even before and after service times. Smoking is not allowed in or near the synagogue building.
  • Children can participate in service downstairs, or they can play or hang out upstairs. There are no babysitters; children should be supervised to a level appropriate to their age. Warning: There is a building under construction in the lot behind the synagogue.
  • In our synagogue everyone stands for every kaddish (even the Mourner's Kaddish. We mention this because the practice of some synagogues is to have only the mourners reciting kaddish to stand.

Getting there

Take the B line to the Washington Street stop, or take the #65 bus to the corner of Washington Street and Euston Street. Continue walking northwest; the synagogue will be on your right.

Ask your hosts, or walk along with them.

Parking

While KTM has a small parking lot, it is typically closed and blocked off on the Sabbath; children often play ball in the lot. If you are shomer Shabbat, and your host does not have a driveway or easily accessible non-resident parking, let us know and we will arrange for you to park in the KTM lot before Shabbat begins.

Otherwise, Brighton has a variety of street parking locations around the synagogue. Here is an approximate map of the area’s parking. Please read the signage carefully; most streets have a combination of visitor parking and resident-sticker-only parking.

Schedule

Wednesday night, Thursday, Friday daytime

The Jewish holiday of Shemini Atzeret begins on Wednesday night, and Simchat Torah begins on Thursday night. Consequently, we will be out of phone or electronic contact starting late Wednesday afternoon. You are welcome to join our synagogue for Shemini Atzeret or Simchat Torah services, but these are not part of the bar mitzvah.

Friday evening

For out-of-town guests, and for shomer shabbat guests who will be staying nearby, we will host a reception on Friday night, followed by Shabbat dinner, at our house. Candle-lighting for Shabbat is at 5:48 p.m.; the reception will begin at 6:30. When you respond to our invitation, please let us know if you will be attending the reception.

This reception, and dessert following havdallah on Saturday night, will be in lieu of the customary bar mitzvah party. A paintball party for Sam’s classmates is in the works.

Saturday morning services

Shacharit begins at 8:45 a.m.; if you want to make sure that you will hear Sam leyn his Torah portion (the first aliyah of Bereshit, the one with the seven days of creation), you should show up by 9:00. Services will run until shortly after noon.

Lunch following services

We'll ask guests to move out of the way or to help as we move chairs to the side and set up some tables for a light kiddush luncheon with the KTM community. Once you've re-entered the room, please wait until someone has made kiddush (the blessing over grape juice). If it's too loud, or you don't know how on earth you'd recognize kiddush even if it was said in your ear, then as a practical matter, take a general “amen!” as a cue to get yourself some lunch. If it is your tradition to wash ritually before eating bread, the sinks are in the rear corner of the women's section.

After people have had a chance to get a little food, Sam will speak about his Torah portion, and one or more parents will speak. Lunch will conclude with bentsching (grace after meals) at some point. Timing is vague because people will hang out as long as they like, and leave as they need to leave.

The rest of the afternoon

Many guests will now disperse to home or hotel. Those who are shomer shabbas or who otherwise want to hang out. If the weather permits, we may also head for a local park/playground.

The rabbi holds a class late Saturday afternoon in the synagogue, followed by mincha services, seudah shlishit, and maariv. Havdallah (the end of Shabbat) will be at about 6:48 p.m., after which guests are invited to join us at our house for dessert.
