C is for Chance

Many people criticize state lotteries as "a tax on people bad at math", a program that takes more from the poor than from the rich, and a way for the government to have a vested interest in compulsive gambling. However, in New Holstein, the lottery is none of these things; instead, it is a bulwark of social stability. Not only are its payouts generous, but participation is mandatory.
